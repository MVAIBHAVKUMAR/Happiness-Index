Did You Know?

  • The psychological impact of exercising, not on a treadmill indoors, but outside in nature, is starting to be demonstrated by the
    science. In a study of adults undergoing Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) for depression, the group whose therapy was
    provided in a forest setting showed a remission rate that was 61 per cent higher than those who underwent the same programme in a hospital setting.

  • The relationship between sleep and mental health works both ways. Prioritizing sleep will help your mental health,
    and making changes to your day will affect your sleep.

  • How you feed your brain influences how you feel. Traditional Mediterranean, Japanese and Norwegian diets show benefits for mental health.

  • Human connection is a powerful tool for stress resilience. Your relationships change your biology and psychology.
